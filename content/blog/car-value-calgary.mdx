## What car value actually means in Alberta market

Car value is the amount a buyer is willing to pay today, not what you paid
before. In Alberta market, value typically falls into two distinct categories:

- **Market value** applies to vehicles that run reliably and can be legally
  resold to end users.
- **Scrap or parts value** applies to vehicles where repair costs exceed resale
  potential.

Unlike private listings where sellers can ask whatever they want, real cash

## Market value vs scrap value in Calgary

Understanding this distinction prevents disappointment and wasted time.

**Market value vehicles**

- Running and safe to drive
- Clean title and usable mileage
- Common demand in Calgary or Airdrie

**Scrap or parts value vehicles**

- Engine or transmission problems
- Accident damage or rust
- Failing inspections or parked long-term

In Calgary market, trucks and SUVs often maintain market value longer due to
consistent demand. Older sedans, especially with mechanical issues, typically
drop to scrap pricing faster than in eastern provinces.

## Online valuation tools: helpful starting point

Most sellers start online, which is reasonable. Tools like Canadian Black Book,
Kelley Blue Book Canada, and AutoTrader.ca are commonly used to estimate used
car value in Alberta. These calculators work reasonably well for clean,
road-ready vehicles with average mileage and no damage. Where they fall short:
they do not account for towing logistics, local resale difficulty, Alberta
specific supply-demand dynamics, or reconditioning costs for damaged vehicles.

For cars with mechanical problems, accident history, or significant wear,
online estimates typically run 20-40% higher than actual cash offers. Treat
these tools as a reference point, not a guaranteed price. This gap is
especially noticeable when estimating scrap car value in Calgary or completing
