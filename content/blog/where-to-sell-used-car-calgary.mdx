## Who buys used cars in Calgary? Your four main options

When selling a car in Calgary, you will usually deal with one of the groups
below. Understanding how each one works makes the decision much easier.
![A person looking for who buy used cars in calgary on his mobile phone](/images/blog/who-buy-used-cars-in-calgary.webp)

### Private sale in Calgary through Kijiji or Facebook

Selling privately means listing your car on a marketplace and meeting buyers
yourself. You control the price, choose your buyer, and keep every dollar from
the sale. But you are doing all the work. That means writing ads, taking photos,
answering messages, meeting strangers for viewings, supervising test drives, and
negotiating prices, often multiple times. You will also handle all paperwork
yourself, from the bill of sale to the registration transfer. This works best
when your car runs well, looks decent, and maximum money matters more than your
time.

### Dealership trade-in

Trading your car at a Calgary dealership makes everything simple. If you are
buying another vehicle from them, they appraise yours and apply that value to
your new purchase. Everything happens in one visit. The appeal is convenience,
no ads, no strangers, no stress. The dealership handles paperwork, and you
drive away in your new car the same day. But you will get significantly less
money. Dealerships typically offer 20-30% below private sale value because they
need a profit margin when they resell your car. This option is best when you
are already buying from that dealer, and simplicity beats getting top dollar.

### Scrap yards

Scrap yards buy vehicles based on metal weight and salvageable parts. They will
take almost anything, even cars that do not run or have severe damage. Typical
payouts range from $200-$800, making this the lowest-value option. However,
towing fees can reduce that total. This option is best for true end-of-life
vehicles with no other realistic selling options. Many sellers in Calgary skip
scrap yards unless the vehicle truly has no other path.

## Paperwork and getting paid

Closing the sale is straightforward once you know what is needed. In Alberta,
requirements are simpler than in many provinces. You will need your vehicle
registration (proves ownership), a basic bill of sale with buyer and seller
details, and your driver license for ID. That is it. No safety inspection is
required in Alberta, unlike Ontario or BC.

Before the buyer or tow truck arrives, do a final check. Look in the glove box,
under the seats, and in the trunk for personal items. People often forget
insurance papers, garage remotes, or sunglasses. Remove your license plates
too. In Alberta, plates stay with you, not the car.

## Frequently asked questions

**Where is the best place to sell a used car in Calgary?**

It depends on your priorities. Private sales (Kijiji, Facebook Marketplace) pay
the most but take weeks. Cash for cars services offer the best speed-to-value
balance, especially for older or damaged vehicles. Scrap yards pay the least
but accept anything.

**Who buys junk cars in Calgary?**

Cash for cars companies and scrap yards both buy junk vehicles in Calgary. Cash
buyers typically pay more because they resell usable parts or export vehicles.
Compare at least two offers before deciding.

**Can I sell a car without a safety inspection in Alberta?**

Yes. Alberta law does not require sellers to provide safety inspection
certificates. You can sell any vehicle "as-is," and buyers handle their own
inspections if desired.

**How fast can I sell my car in Calgary?**

Cash for cars services complete most sales within 24-48 hours from first
contact to payment. Private sales typically take 3-6 weeks. Dealership trade-ins
happen the same day, only when buying another vehicle.

**Do I need to clean my car before selling?**

For private sales, presentation matters. For cash buyers, no. They buy vehicles
in any condition, including dirty, damaged, or non-running cars.

**What if I still owe money on my car?**

You must pay off the loan before selling. Contact your lender for the payoff
amount. Some cash buyers can pay lenders directly and give you the difference,
but most prefer clear titles.
