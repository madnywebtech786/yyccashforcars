## Calgary Winters Are Hard on Cars. We All Know This.

There's something about this city specifically that just destroys vehicles over time. The freeze-thaw cycles eat through the undercarriage. The road salt gets into everything. One bad winter can turn a car that was running fine in October into something that won't start by March.

And then what? You've got a car that's not worth fixing — because the repair bill would cost more than the car itself — but you feel weird just abandoning it. So it sits. On your driveway, in your backyard, taking up a garage spot that could actually be useful.

Meanwhile, that car is still worth something. Maybe not as a running vehicle. But the steel, the aluminum, the copper wiring, the salvageable parts — all of that has real market value. And right now in Calgary there are buyers actively looking for exactly what's sitting on your property.

Here's what I wish someone had told me earlier — you don't have to do anything to the car to sell it.

You don't have to fix it. Don't clean it. Don't get it inspected. Don't worry about whether it starts. You genuinely do not have to touch it at all. The whole point of junk car removal is that they take it exactly as it is, wherever it is, in whatever condition it's in.

Got a car with no wheels? Fine. Engine missing? Fine. Sat outside through six Calgary winters and looks like it belongs in a post-apocalyptic film? Also fine.

The scrap value comes from weight and materials, not from whether the thing could pass a safety inspection. Not bad for something that was just taking up space.

This is the question that holds people back more than anything else. They assume their car is in too bad a shape to be worth anything. So let me just clear this up once and for all. These are all cars that qualify for cash removal in Calgary:

If it's made of metal and it's sitting on your property, there is a very good chance someone in Calgary will pay you cash to take it away today. The condition matters far less than most people think.

## Do This Before the Truck Shows Up

Walk through the car before they arrive. Seriously, just take five minutes and do it. People leave things behind constantly and then regret it. Before you hand over the keys, make sure you have grabbed:

- Any personal documents from the glovebox like registration or insurance papers
- Prescription sunglasses, chargers, cash, or anything stored in the console
- Kids' belongings, winter gear, or tools from the trunk
- Any aftermarket parts you paid for and want to keep like custom rims or a stereo

Once the car drives away on that flatbed it's not coming back. Five minutes now saves a lot of frustration later.
