### Step 1: Check your car real condition

Before you can sell your car, you need to know what you are working with. Be
honest with yourself. Buyers in Calgary know their stuff, especially when it
comes to winter wear and tear.
![Check your car real condition](/images/blog/inspecting-car-before-selling-in-canada.png)


**Exterior check**

Walk around your vehicle and look for:

- Dents, scratches, or rust (especially under doors and wheel wells)
- Hail damage (very common in Alberta)
- Tire condition and tread depth
- Cracked or chipped windshield
- Faded or peeling paint

**Interior assessment**

Sit inside and notice:

- Seat stains or tears
- Dashboard warning lights
- Odors from smoke, pets, or moisture
- Working climate control and electronics
- Overall cleanliness

**Mechanical condition**

Ask yourself:

- Does it start reliably in cold weather?
- Any strange noises when driving?
- How do the brakes feel?
- Any fluid leaks under the car?
- When was the last oil change?


**Documentation**

Gather these if you have them:

- Service records and receipts
- Vehicle registration
- Owner manual
- Any warranty information

Cars with a complete service history sell faster and for more money, about
10-15% more, according to industry data.

### Step 2: Figure out what your car is worth

Pricing matters more than almost anything else. Too high and nobody calls. Too
low and you lose money.

**Use Canadian valuation tools**

Check these trusted sources:

- Canadian Black Book
- Kelley Blue Book Canada
- CARFAX Canada for vehicles with history reports

These give you a baseline, but remember Calgary market has its own personality.

**Research local listings**

Spend 20 minutes searching:

- AutoTrader.ca
- Kijiji Autos
- Facebook Marketplace

Look for cars like yours (same year, make, model, similar mileage) listed in
Calgary, Airdrie, Okotoks, or Chestermere. Note what they are asking and how
long they have been listed.

**Calgary market reality**

Our Alberta market values:

- AWD and 4WD vehicles hold value
- Winter tires add value
- Heated seats and remote start matter
- Trucks and SUVs stay in demand
- Hail damage lowers value fast

### Step 3: Prepare your vehicle for sale

You do not need to make your car perfect, but a clean vehicle makes a huge
difference in how buyers see it.

**Basic cleaning**

- Wash and dry the exterior thoroughly
- Vacuum all interior surfaces
- Clean windows inside and out
- Remove all personal items
- Wipe down the dashboard

Professional detailing costs $150-250 in Calgary, but can increase your sale
price by $500 or more.

**Small fixes worth making**

- Replace any burnt-out bulbs
- Fix windshield chips before they crack
- Top up all fluids
- Inflate tires to the proper pressure

## The used car selling process: Private sale edition

If you decide to sell privately, here is the realistic process.

### Creating your listing

Take 12-15 clear photos in good light:

- Front, back, and both sides
- Interior shots (front and back seats)
- Dashboard showing mileage
- Engine bay
- Any damage or wear

Write an honest description including:

- Year, make, model, trim level
- Actual mileage
- Key features (AWD, heated seats, backup camera)
- Maintenance history
- Known issues (be upfront)
- Why you are selling

### Handling buyers safely

Calgary is generally safe, but use common sense:

- Meet in public places during daylight
- Bring someone with you
- Never let strangers test drive alone
- Check their driver license and insurance
- Trust your gut. If something feels off, it probably is

### Negotiating the price

Most buyers will negotiate. That is normal. Know your bottom line before
meeting anyone. Be prepared to justify your price with facts, recent
maintenance, low mileage, clean history, or market comparisons.

## Alberta-specific rules you need to know

Selling a car in Alberta is actually easier than in most provinces, but you
still need to follow the rules. The used car ownership transfer in Alberta is
simple compared to other provinces, which makes selling a used car in Canada
easier here.

### No safety inspection required

Good news: Unlike Ontario or BC, Alberta does not require sellers to provide a
safety certificate. You can sell a vehicle "as-is" and let the buyer handle any
inspections.

### Bill of sale requirements

Create a simple bill of sale, including:

- Date of sale
- Your name and address (seller)
- Buyer name and address
- Vehicle details (VIN, year, make, model)
- Odometer reading
- Sale price
- "Sold as-is" statement
- Both signatures

Keep a copy for your records.

### Registration transfer

In Alberta:

- Sign the back of your vehicle registration
- Remove your license plates (they stay with you, not the car)
- Give the signed registration to the buyer
- The buyer has 14 days to register it in their name

Important: Cancel your insurance and registration right after the sale. Until
the buyer registers it, you are technically still the owner if tickets or
issues come up.

### Dealing with liens

If you still owe money on your car, you must pay off the loan before selling.
Contact your lender for the payoff amount. Never try to sell a car with an
outstanding lien; it is illegal, and the buyer cannot register it.

## Getting paid safely

Payment scams are common. Protect yourself.

**Best payment methods**

- Bank draft or certified check - Meet at the buyer bank and watch them get it
  from the teller
- Cash (for amounts under $5,000) - Count it carefully and meet at your bank
- E-transfer (for amounts under $10,000) - Confirm it is in your account before
  handing over keys

**Never accept**

- Personal checks (they can bounce weeks later)
- Money orders from strangers
- PayPal or Venmo
- Any "overpayment" scenario
